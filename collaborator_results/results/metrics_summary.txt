=======================================================
Test-set evaluation summary — collaborator's trained model
562 pairs, Sony SID test split (full coverage)
=======================================================

PSNR (dB)  — higher is better
  mean 26.07   std 3.30   min 17.88   max 33.46
  Good score: >28 dB is strong for this task (SID-style extreme low-light
  enhancement); 24-28 dB is solid; <20 dB is weak. GANs typically trade a
  few dB of PSNR for more realistic texture vs. a pure-reconstruction model.

SSIM  — [0,1], higher is better
  mean 0.711   std 0.151   min 0.254   max 0.939
  Good score: >0.85 excellent, 0.7-0.85 good, 0.5-0.7 moderate, <0.5 weak.

LPIPS  — perceptual distance, lower is better
  mean 0.492   std 0.161   min 0.164   max 0.829
  Good score: <0.2 very close perceptually, 0.2-0.4 good, 0.4-0.6 moderate,
  >0.6 weak. Values in the 0.4-0.5 range are common on extreme low-light
  enhancement since noise/texture differences are unavoidable even in
  visually good reconstructions.

LOE  — Lightness Order Error vs. the original dark input, lower is better
  mean 322.66   std 225.12   min 68.25   max 944.81
  Good score: a few hundred is good (few unnatural local contrast
  reversals); >1000 signals the enhancement is distorting relative
  brightness ordering. Wide std here reflects scene difficulty varying a
  lot across the test set (min 68 to max 945).

FID  — set-level (one number for all 562 images), lower is better
  172.90
  Good score: <50 is strong, 50-100 reasonable, >100 weak — BUT: FID needs
  a large sample to be reliable (literature standard is 2000+ images); with
  562 images this number is indicative only, not a paper-comparable score.
